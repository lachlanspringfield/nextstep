Aged Care Quality Standards

Ensuring the best care for older Australians

1. Consumer Dignity and Choice

Consumers are treated with dignity and respect and can make choices about their care, ensuring their identity, culture, and diversity are valued.

2. Ongoing Assessment and Planning with Consumers

Consumers participate in ongoing assessments and care planning to ensure their changing needs, goals, and preferences are met.

3. Personal and Clinical Care

Consumers receive safe, effective, and quality care tailored to their needs and delivered with kindness and respect.

4. Services and Supports for Daily Living

Consumers are supported to live as independently as possible and maintain a meaningful lifestyle through services that enhance their wellbeing.

5. Organisation’s Service Environment

The environment is safe, comfortable, clean, and suitable for consumers’ needs while promoting independence.

6. Feedback and Complaints

Consumers feel empowered to provide feedback and complaints without fear of retribution and are involved in the resolution process.

7. Human Resources

The workforce is skilled, qualified, and adequately trained to provide care that aligns with the standards and the needs of consumers.

8. Organisational Governance

Organisations maintain effective governance frameworks, ensure compliance, and continuously improve care quality and safety.
